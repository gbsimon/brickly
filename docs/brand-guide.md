# Brickly -- Brand Guide (Playful + Friendly)

## Brand Essence
- Promise: Rebuild LEGO sets faster with a calm, joyful checklist.
- Personality: Playful, friendly, helpful, tidy.
- Feelings: Satisfying progress, light momentum, no-stress organization.

## Voice Principles
- Cheerful, not childish
- Clear and concise, never fussy
- Encouraging, not bossy
- Tiny wink of fun, never gimmicky

## Tone Spectrum
- Default: bright and helpful
- When users are stuck: calm and reassuring
- When users finish: celebratory but short

## Writing Style
- Use short sentences and active voice
- Prefer everyday words over jargon
- Keep "LEGO" capitalized and consistent
- Use "set" and "parts" more than "inventory"

## Do / Don't
- Do: "Let's find the last few parts."
- Do: "Nice! You're almost there."
- Do: "Keep building, we'll keep count."
- Don't: "You must complete inventory reconciliation."
- Don't: "Error 500. Please retry."

## Brand Phrases
- "Build without the chaos."
- "Find parts faster."
- "Keep the build moving."
- "Tap, track, done."

## Microcopy Snippets
- Empty state: "No sets here yet. Want to add one?"
- Loading: "Lining up the parts..."
- Syncing: "Catching up across devices..."
- Offline: "Offline, but still building."
- Error: "Oops. That didn't click. Try again?"

## CTA Style
- Primary CTA: short, action-forward
- Examples: "Start a set", "Build smarter", "Try Brickly"

## Accessibility & Clarity
- Avoid idioms that don't translate well
- Use positive language for errors
- Keep instructions short for quick scanning
